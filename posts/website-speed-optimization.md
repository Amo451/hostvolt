In 2021, Google made it official: Core Web Vitals are a ranking factor. That means your server's response time, your page's load speed, and how quickly your content becomes interactive all directly affect where you appear in search results.

## How Hosting Affects Your Rankings

Google measures three main things: how quickly the server responds (TTFB), how fast the largest element loads (LCP), and how long before the page becomes interactive. A slow server creates a cascading effect on all three.

> A 1-second delay in page load time results in 11% fewer pageviews, 16% lower customer satisfaction, and 7% fewer conversions.

## What a Good TTFB Looks Like

- **Kinsta:** average 187ms TTFB — excellent
- **Cloudways:** average 218ms TTFB — excellent
- **SiteGround:** average 412ms TTFB — good
- **A2 Hosting:** average 320ms TTFB — good

## How to Check Your Current Speed

1. Go to PageSpeed Insights and enter your URL
2. Check your TTFB score in the diagnostics section
3. Run the same test from GTmetrix for a second opinion
4. Use Google Search Console Core Web Vitals report for real-world data

## Quick Wins Before Switching Hosts

- Enable server-side caching in your control panel
- Install a caching plugin like WP Rocket or W3 Total Cache
- Enable Cloudflare's free CDN — can cut load times by 30–50%
- Compress and resize images — the number one cause of slow LCP
