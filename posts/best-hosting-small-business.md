Choosing hosting for a small business is different from picking a plan for a personal blog. Downtime means lost revenue. A slow checkout page means abandoned carts. When something breaks, you need support that actually fixes the problem.

## What Small Businesses Actually Need

- **Uptime above everything** — consistent 99.9%+ uptime, not just advertised claims
- **Email hosting included** — professional email (you@yourbusiness.com) is non-negotiable
- **Easy control panel** — you shouldn't need a developer to manage hosting
- **Scalability** — can you upgrade quickly if your site takes off?
- **Security features** — free SSL, malware scanning, automatic backups

## 1. SiteGround Business Plan

[SiteGround's Business plan](https://siteground.com) is our top all-round option for small businesses. It includes free SSL, daily backups, a CDN, unlimited email accounts, and the fastest support we've encountered at this price point.

## 2. Bluehost Choice Plus

For WordPress businesses, [Bluehost's Choice Plus](https://bluehost.com) is hard to beat on value. Unlimited websites, free domain privacy, and CodeGuard daily backups are all included.

## 3. Cloudways (For Growing Businesses)

Once you're past the startup phase and getting serious traffic, [Cloudways](https://cloudways.com) is worth the higher price. The managed cloud infrastructure means enterprise-level performance without needing a sysadmin.

<div class="callout"><div class="callout-title">📈 When to Consider Cloudways</div><p>If your site generates revenue and you're getting more than 20,000 monthly visitors, the performance Cloudways delivers will more than pay for the higher monthly cost in conversions alone.</p></div>

## Our Recommendation

For most small businesses: **SiteGround Business**. Budget-conscious startups: **Hostinger Business**. High-traffic or e-commerce: **Cloudways**.
